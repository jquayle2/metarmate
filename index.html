Features

Decision support.
Not just data.

MetarMate interprets aviation weather the way an experienced pilot would — telling you what conditions mean for your flight, not just what the numbers say.

🌐
Plain English METAR Decoding
Raw METAR strings translated into pilot-readable summaries. No more mentally parsing 10BKN025 at 6 AM before a flight.
🎨
Flight Category Color Coding
VFR green · MVFR blue · IFR red · LIFR magenta. Consistent with FAA sectional chart conventions — the colors you already know mean something.
💨
Wind Analysis
Sustained and gust components color-coded at operationally meaningful thresholds. Orange caution, red warning — never ambiguous.
📣
Pilot Notes
Automatic flagging of conditions that matter — low temp/dewpoint spread, gusts above threshold, deteriorating trends. What a CFI would circle on a weather printout.
🔮
TAF Verification
Compares what the forecast promised vs. what actually happened. Per-metric scoring on wind, ceiling, and visibility. Know when a model is running optimistic.
🏔
Density Altitude
Calculated with performance impact language calibrated for your environment. Critical for hot/high operations — pilots in the desert need real numbers.
📍
Nearest Airport Search
Location-based nearest airports with SwiftData-backed favorites. Fastest-accessed airports always at the top.
🎤
Siri Integration
"Hey Siri, what's the weather at KVGT?" Hands-free weather briefings via App Intents — for when you're already strapped in.
🗺
Advisory Weather for Uncontrolled Strips
Thousands of airports have no official METAR. MetarMate fills the gap with modeled estimates — clearly marked advisory, never confused with official data.

What Sets It Apart

Built for gaps other apps ignore.

Capabilities that don't exist anywhere else in this combination.

Unique Feature
Weather for Airports With No METAR
Thousands of grass strips, backcountry airstrips, and small uncontrolled airports have no official weather reporting. MetarMate uses modeled weather to give you an estimated flight category with full conditions — clearly marked advisory so you never mistake it for official data.
Smart Analysis
TAF Verification Scoring
Know how well the forecast performed against what actually happened. Per-metric scoring on wind, ceiling, and visibility shows whether conditions were better or worse than predicted — measured as operational surprise, not just raw math.
Safety First
Pilot-Safe Color Conventions
Red means danger. Not "highlight." Not "selected." MetarMate uses FAA-consistent color conventions throughout — because in aviation, colors need to mean the same thing every time you look.
Siri Ready
Hands-Free Briefings
Ask Siri for a weather briefing at any airport while you're preflight or walking to the aircraft. App Intents integration delivers the most operationally relevant summary without touching your phone.
How It Works

Open. Tap. Brief.

No setup. No account. No weather jargon required.

01
Find Your Airport
Nearest airports load automatically based on your location. Search by name, ICAO, or IATA code. Save favorites for instant access.
02
See the Picture
Flight category, wind, visibility, ceiling — decoded into plain English the moment you tap. Color-coded at a glance, detailed when you need it.
03
Read the Trend
Observed conditions over time, forecast verification score, and TAF deviation summary. Know if conditions are improving or deteriorating before you go.
04
Make the Call
Pilot Notes surface the conditions that affect your decision. MetarMate doesn't make the go/no-go call — but it gives you everything you need to make it confidently.
Mission

Built by a pilot.

General aviation pilots deserve weather tools that think the way they do. The raw METAR format exists for machines — MetarMate exists to translate it back into language that informs real decisions under real pressure.

Every feature in MetarMate started from a genuine operational need: the density altitude that catches you by surprise at a hot desert strip, the TAF that was optimistic about the morning fog, the grass runway that has no official weather reporting at all. These are problems pilots actually face. MetarMate is built around solving them.
Privacy

Your data stays on your device.

MetarMate does not collect, sell, or share personal data. Weather is fetched from public FAA and Open-Meteo APIs. Favorites and preferences are stored locally using SwiftData and never transmitted off your device. No accounts. No analytics. No ads — ever.
